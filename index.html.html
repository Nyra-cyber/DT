Across the Mountain of Swords and the Sea of Fire

Written by Mya Than Tint

Imagine waking up on a deserted and hostile island after a disastrous shipwreck.
No food. No tools. No connection to civilization.
Only three strangers—and none of you trust one another.

Across the Mountain of Swords and the Sea of Fire is not simply a survival story.
It is a powerful examination of human nature, fear and the difficult choice between selfishness and cooperation.

The novel places four completely different individuals together and forces them to confront both the island and themselves.

People You Will Meet

  • Than Chaung — a hardworking laborer who understands survival through action.
  • Ko Nanda — an educated intellectual whose knowledge is tested by reality.
  • U Ba Yan — a businessman who sees value through profit and possession.
  • Ye Myint — a rebellious young man struggling with anger and direction.

Why This Story Stands Out

  1. Survival becomes a test of personality and values.
  2. Characters slowly break down social barriers.
  3. The story asks what truly makes people human.

At first, fear turns everyone inward.
People argue, protect themselves and refuse to trust.
But slowly, necessity changes them.
They begin to understand that surviving alone may not be possible.

The novel explores how people can overcome pride, fear and selfishness to create something greater together.
